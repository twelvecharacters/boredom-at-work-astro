You've mastered the basics of 3D printing. You've printed your Benchy, made some cable clips, and organized your desk. Now you're thinking: "What else can I actually do with this thing?"

The answer: Way more than you think.

3D printing isn't just about making plastic organizers. It's about creating things that are impossible to buy, solving problems nobody else has, and making objects that genuinely impress people.

Here are 30 truly interesting things to 3D print - from practical innovations to mind-bending art pieces.

## Pro Tips for Interesting Prints

### Design Considerations

1. **Think in layers** - Design objects that showcase 3D printing's strengths (complex geometry, internal structures)
2. **Embrace customization** - The real value is making things impossible to buy
3. **Consider multi-material** - Combine flexible and rigid filaments for unique properties

### Material Selection

- **PLA:** Great for decorative items, prototypes
- **PETG:** Better for functional parts, outdoor use
- **TPU:** Flexible parts, grips, phone cases
- **Wood/Metal PLA:** Aesthetic upgrades for display pieces

### Finishing Techniques

- **Sanding & painting:** Transform prints into professional-looking objects
- **Vapor smoothing:** ABS and ASA can be smoothed with acetone vapor
- **Multi-color printing:** Use filament swaps or multi-material systems

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Don't Print Just to Print

Every print should have a purpose - functional, educational, or aesthetic. Random trinkets lose their appeal fast.

### Start with Proven Designs

Before modifying or designing, print the original. Understand why it works before changing it.

### Consider Print Time vs. Value

A 40-hour print better be worth it. Sometimes a 2-hour print delivers more value.

### Respect Intellectual Property

Don't print and sell copyrighted characters or trademarked items. Stick to original designs or properly licensed models.
