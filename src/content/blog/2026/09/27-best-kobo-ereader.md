Kobo is the strongest alternative to the Kindle, and for some readers it is the better choice: it has built-in library borrowing, native EPUB support, and physical page-turn buttons that Amazon no longer offers on any Kindle. But Kobo's lineup can be just as confusing as Amazon's. Here is which model to buy.

(Deciding between the two brands first? Read our [Kindle vs Kobo comparison](/kindle-vs-kobo/) and the head-to-head [Kindle Paperwhite vs Kobo Clara BW](/kindle-paperwhite-vs-kobo-clara/).)

## The Quick Answer

- **Best overall:** Kobo Libra Colour ($229.99). Buttons, color, waterproof.
- **Best value:** Kobo Clara BW ($139.99).
- **Color on a budget:** Kobo Clara Colour ($159.99).
- **Note-taking:** Kobo Elipsa 2E (large screen with stylus).

If you want one pick, the **Libra Colour** is the Kobo to get for most people.

## Kobo Clara BW ($139.99): The Value Pick

The Clara BW is Kobo's compact, black-and-white reader, and it is the one to buy if you want the Kobo experience for the least money. You get a 6-inch 300 ppi screen, waterproofing, an adjustable warm light, and Kobo's excellent library and EPUB support.

**Buy it if:** you read mostly novels and want a small, affordable reader that still borrows library books with ease.

## Kobo Clara Colour ($159.99): Color on a Budget

The Clara Colour is the same compact 6-inch body with a color e-ink screen added. For just $20 more than the Clara BW, it is worth it if you read any comics, magazines, or illustrated content.

**Buy it if:** you want color without spending much. Color e-ink is not as sharp for pure text, but for mixed reading it is a nice upgrade.

## Kobo Libra Colour ($229.99): The Best Overall

The Libra Colour is where Kobo really separates itself from Kindle. You get a larger 7-inch color screen, waterproofing, and, crucially, **physical page-turn buttons**, which no current Kindle has since the Oasis was discontinued.

**Buy it if:** you want the best all-around Kobo, especially if one-handed reading with buttons matters to you. This is our recommended pick for most Kobo buyers.

## Kobo Sage and Elipsa 2E: Bigger Screens and Notes

The Sage (8-inch) and Elipsa 2E (10.3-inch) both support a stylus for note-taking and marking up documents, similar to the Kindle Scribe. The Elipsa 2E's large screen makes it the Kobo for people who want to read and write on one device.

**Buy one if:** you want handwriting and annotation on a Kobo. For most pure readers, they are more than you need.

## Why Choose Kobo at All?

Three reasons Kobo beats Kindle for the right reader:

- **Library borrowing** is built in through OverDrive, no separate app needed.
- **Native EPUB support** and better [format flexibility](/best-e-readers/) for books from anywhere.
- **Physical page-turn buttons** on several models.

The tradeoffs are a smaller bookstore than Amazon's and lower resale value. If those matter more to you, a [Kindle Paperwhite](/kindle-paperwhite-review/) may be the better call.

## The Bottom Line

For most people the **Kobo Libra Colour** ($229.99) is the best Kobo, thanks to its buttons, color screen, and waterproofing.
