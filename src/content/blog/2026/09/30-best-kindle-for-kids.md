A Kindle is one of the best ways to get a child reading more, without the games, videos, and endless distractions of a tablet. But Amazon sells several Kids editions, and it is not obvious which one is right. Here is how to choose.

(For the grown-up models and the full range, see [which Kindle should you buy](/which-kindle-should-you-buy/). Buying for an older student instead? Our [best Kindle for students](/best-kindle-for-students/) guide covers that.)

## The Quick Answer

- **Best for most kids:** Kindle Kids ($129.99).
- **Bigger, waterproof screen:** Kindle Paperwhite Kids ($179.99).
- **Younger kids who want color:** Kindle Colorsoft Kids ($269.99).

If you want one pick: the **Kindle Kids**. It covers what almost every family needs.

## What a "Kids Edition" Actually Is

This trips a lot of parents up, so it is worth being clear: a Kids edition is the exact same hardware as the regular model, bundled with extras aimed at families. For about $20 more than the standard version, you get:

- A **kid-proof cover** in fun colors
- A **2-year worry-free guarantee** (if it breaks, Amazon replaces it, no questions asked)
- **Parental controls** set up out of the box
- **Amazon Kids+**, Amazon's subscription of kid-friendly books and content (a period is typically included)

The worry-free guarantee alone often justifies the small price bump, given how kids treat electronics.

## Kindle Kids ($129.99): Best for Most

The standard [Kindle](/which-kindle-should-you-buy/) Kids is the right choice for the majority of families. It has the same sharp 300 ppi screen as the base model, it is small and light for little hands, and the bundle covers everything you need to hand it over and relax.

**Buy it if:** you want a simple, affordable, distraction-free reader for a child, without overthinking it.

## Kindle Paperwhite Kids ($179.99): Bigger and Waterproof

The Paperwhite Kids steps up to a larger 7-inch screen, waterproofing, and an adjustable warm light. The bigger screen fits more text, the warm light helps with bedtime reading, and waterproofing survives spills and bathtub reading.

**Buy it if:** your child reads a lot, reads at bedtime, or is likely to get the device wet. It is the best Kids edition for keen young readers.

## Kindle Colorsoft Kids ($269.99): Color for Younger Readers

The Colorsoft Kids adds a color screen, which makes a real difference for younger children reading picture-heavy books, early readers, and illustrated titles. It is the most expensive option, so it only makes sense if color genuinely matters for your child's reading stage.

**Buy it if:** you are buying for a younger child whose books are full of color.

## Kindle vs a Tablet for Kids

If you are torn between a Kindle and a cheap tablet, remember what each is for. A Kindle does one thing: books. No games, no video, no ads, no notifications, and an e-ink screen that is calmer for young eyes with weeks of battery life.

A tablet does more, but that "more" is exactly the distraction you are usually trying to avoid with a young reader. For reading specifically, the Kindle wins. Our [Kindle vs iPad for reading](/kindle-vs-ipad-reading/) comparison goes deeper if you want the full argument.

## A Cheaper Alternative

You do not strictly need a Kids edition. You can buy any standard Kindle, add a [protective case](/best-kindle-cases-2026/), and set up parental controls yourself in the settings. You lose the extended worry-free guarantee and the bundled Amazon Kids+, but you can save a little money if you already have a spare Kindle or catch a deal on a regular model.

## The Bottom Line

For most families, the **Kindle Kids** ($129.99) is the best buy: simple, affordable, and built to survive a child. Step up to the **Paperwhite Kids** ($179.99) for a keen reader who wants a bigger, waterproof screen, or the **Colorsoft Kids** ($269.99) for a younger child who reads in color.
