Both the Kindle Scribe and the reMarkable let you read and write on a large e-ink screen with a stylus. They look similar in photos. But they are built around very different ideas, and picking the wrong one is an expensive mistake. Here is how to choose.

(If you are not sure a big writing tablet is what you need at all, our [Kindle Scribe review](/kindle-scribe-review/) covers whether it is worth it just for work notes, and [which Kindle should you buy](/which-kindle-should-you-buy/) covers the smaller reading-focused models.)

## The Quick Answer

- **You want to read and write on one device:** Kindle Scribe (from $429.99).
- **Writing is the priority and you want the best paper feel:** reMarkable 2 (from $379).
- **You want color notes and a front light:** reMarkable Paper Pro ($579).

For most people, the **Scribe** is the more sensible buy, because it is a genuinely great e-reader that also takes notes, while the reMarkable is a genuinely great notebook that reads poorly.

## Price and Lineup

| Device | Price | Screen | Color | Front Light |
|--------|-------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Kindle Scribe | from $429.99 | ~11" | No | Yes |
| reMarkable 2 | from $379 | 10.3" | No | No |
| reMarkable Paper Pro | $579 | 11.8" | Yes | Yes |

Note that the reMarkable Marker is an added cost on some configurations, and the Paper Pro's Marker Plus is $50 on top of the tablet. Amazon includes a pen with the Scribe.

## Reading: The Scribe Wins Easily

This is the biggest practical difference. The Kindle Scribe is a full Kindle. You get Amazon's enormous ebook store, [Kindle Unlimited](/kindle-unlimited-worth-it/), Whispersync, and a built-in warm front light for night reading.

The reMarkable is a notebook that can display documents and side-loaded books, but there is no major bookstore behind it, the reading experience is basic, and the reMarkable 2 has no front light, so it is awkward in a dim room. If reading is any part of your plan, the Scribe is the clear choice.

## Writing: The reMarkable's Home Turf

If you flip the priority to writing, the picture changes. The reMarkable has a famously paper-like writing feel, a faster, more responsive pen (especially on the Paper Pro), and a clean, distraction-free interface built entirely around note-taking, sketching, and marking up PDFs.

The Scribe is very good at notes too, and it keeps them alongside your books. But dedicated note-takers often prefer the reMarkable's texture and focus. If handwriting is 80% of what you will do, the reMarkable earns its place.

## Ecosystem and the Subscription Question

The Scribe lives inside Amazon: your books, notes, and documents sync through your existing account with nothing extra to pay.

reMarkable leans on its **Connect subscription** for the best experience, including handwriting-to-text conversion, unlimited cloud sync, and full mobile and desktop apps. You can use the device without it, but the company pushes it hard, and it is a recurring cost the Scribe does not ask for. Factor that into the long-term price.

## Color

Only the reMarkable Paper Pro offers a color screen, which is genuinely useful if you annotate in color or sketch. No Kindle Scribe has color yet. If color notes matter to you, the Paper Pro is the only option here, though it is the most expensive device in the comparison.

## Which Should You Buy?

- **Read books and take notes on one device:** Kindle Scribe (from $429.99). The best all-rounder and the best value.
- **Serious note-taker who wants the best writing feel:** reMarkable 2 (from $379), as long as you can live without a front light and a bookstore.
- **You want color notes and a light:** reMarkable Paper Pro ($579).
- **You mostly just read:** neither. Get a [Kindle Paperwhite](/kindle-paperwhite-review/) and save hundreds.

For most readers of this site, who want to read a lot and jot the occasional note, the Scribe is the right answer.
