Amazon's Kindle lineup has quietly grown into one of the more confusing gadget ranges out there. There is a base Kindle, a Paperwhite, a Paperwhite Signature Edition, a Colorsoft, a couple of Kids editions, and the note-taking Scribe, each in more than one configuration. If you have decided you want a Kindle but have no idea which one, this guide is for you.

(Still deciding between brands? Read our [Kindle vs Kobo comparison](/kindle-vs-kobo/) first, or our full [best e-readers guide](/best-e-readers/) that covers every option. And if you are not sure an e-reader is right for you at all, start with [Is a Kindle Worth It?](/is-kindle-worth-it/))

Let's cut through it.

## The Quick Answer

- **Most people:** Kindle Paperwhite ($159.99). The sweet spot.
- **Tight budget:** Base Kindle ($109.99). Same great reading, fewer comforts.
- **Comics, color, magazines:** Kindle Colorsoft (from $249.99).
- **Writing and note-taking:** Kindle Scribe (from $429.99).
- **For a child:** a Kids edition (base Kids $129.99, Paperwhite Kids $179.99).

If you read one line and leave: get the **Paperwhite**. It is the right answer for the large majority of readers.

## The Base Kindle ($109.99): The Budget Pick

The entry-level [Kindle](https://amazon.com/kindle) is small, light, and cheaper than everything else. You get a 6-inch 300 ppi screen (the same sharpness as the pricier models), USB-C charging, and access to the entire Kindle store.

**Buy it if:** you mostly read plain text novels, want to spend as little as possible, and read indoors in decent lighting.

**Skip it if:** you read at night, in the bath, or outside. It has no adjustable warm light and no waterproofing, which are exactly the two things that make the Paperwhite so much more pleasant for real-world reading.

## The Kindle Paperwhite ($159.99): The One to Buy

The [Paperwhite](https://amazon.com/kindle) is the model most people should get, and it is not close. For about $50 more than the base Kindle, you get:

- A bigger **7-inch** display with faster page turns
- **IPX8 waterproofing** (survives a drop in the tub or pool)
- An **adjustable warm light** for comfortable night reading
- Battery life measured in **weeks**, not days

That combination is why the Paperwhite is our default recommendation across the site. It removes the small daily annoyances of the base model without pushing into premium territory.

If you want a little more, the **Paperwhite Signature Edition** ($199.99) adds 32GB of storage, an auto-adjusting front light, and wireless charging. Nice to have, but not essential. Most people are happier keeping the $40 and buying a good [Kindle case](/best-kindle-cases-2026/) instead.

## The Kindle Colorsoft (from $249.99): For Color Content

The Colorsoft is the Kindle for people whose reading is not just black text on a page. It has a 7-inch color e-ink display, so [comics](/best-e-readers/), graphic novels, magazines, cookbooks, and illustrated or technical books actually look the way they were meant to.

The tradeoff is price and, to a small degree, sharpness. Color e-ink is not as crisp for pure text as the black-and-white screens, and you pay roughly $90 more than the Paperwhite. A Signature Edition of the Colorsoft steps up to 32GB and adds wireless charging.

**Buy it if:** color genuinely matters to what you read. **Skip it if:** you read mostly novels. For a deeper look, see our dedicated [Kindle Colorsoft vs Paperwhite](/kindle-colorsoft-vs-paperwhite/) breakdown.

## The Kindle Scribe (from $429.99): For Writing, Not Just Reading

The [Scribe](/kindle-scribe-review/) is a different kind of device. It pairs a large writable display (around 11 inches) with a pen, so you can take handwritten notes, keep journals, and mark up documents and PDFs directly on the screen. It is the closest thing Amazon makes to a paper notebook.

As a pure reader it is excellent but overkill, and its size makes it less comfortable to hold one-handed than a Paperwhite. There is also a newer Scribe with a color panel at the top of the range for people who want color and note-taking together.

**Buy it if:** you want to write and annotate, not just read. **Skip it if:** you only read books. Our full [Kindle Scribe review](/kindle-scribe-review/) covers whether it is worth it for work notes.

## Buying for a Child

Every Kids edition is the same hardware as its regular counterpart, bundled with a cover, a longer worry-free guarantee, and parental controls, usually for about $20 more:

- **Kindle Kids:** $129.99
- **Kindle Paperwhite Kids:** $179.99
- **Kindle Colorsoft Kids:** $269.99

For most kids, the base Kids edition is plenty. Step up to Paperwhite Kids if they will read in the bath or at bedtime, since the warm light and waterproofing help. There is more detail in our guide to the [best Kindle for students](/best-kindle-for-students/).

## What About the Oasis?

Amazon discontinued the Kindle Oasis in 2025, so it is no longer part of the lineup. Its signature feature, physical page-turn buttons, is not available on any current Kindle. If buttons are a dealbreaker for you, that is one of the strongest reasons to look at [Kobo instead](/kindle-paperwhite-vs-kobo-clara/), which still offers them on several models.

## So, Which Kindle Should You Buy?

- **You want the best Kindle for the money:** Paperwhite ($159.99).
- **You are on a tight budget:** base Kindle ($109.99).
- **You read comics, magazines, or color content:** Colorsoft (from $249.99).
- **You want to take notes and annotate:** Scribe (from $429.99).
- **You are buying for a child:** the matching Kids edition.
