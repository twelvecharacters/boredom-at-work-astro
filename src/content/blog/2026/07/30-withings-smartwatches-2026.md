## The Appeal of the Hybrid Smartwatch

Before we dissect the individual models, it is important to understand why the hybrid smartwatch form factor has endured. When companies like Apple and Samsung release new watches every single year with brighter screens and faster processors, why do people still flock to a watch with real hands and a tiny monochrome OLED display?

The first and most obvious answer is battery life. Traditional smartwatches require charging every single day, or perhaps every two days if you are lucky. This constant need for power creates anxiety and often leads to gaps in your health data when you inevitably forget to place the watch on its charging puck. Withings watches, by contrast, offer up to thirty days of battery life on a single charge. You can wear the watch for an entire month, tracking your sleep every night and your heart rate every day, without ever thinking about a charger.

The second reason is aesthetic appeal. A classic analog watch is a piece of jewelry. It has a timeless quality that a digital screen simply cannot replicate. Withings has mastered the art of hiding technology in plain sight. From a distance, nobody knows you are wearing a piece of advanced health technology. It just looks like you have excellent taste in traditional timepieces.

Finally, there is the concept of digital minimalism. We already spend countless hours staring at our phones, our computer monitors, and our televisions. Do we really need another screen constantly vying for our attention? Withings watches provide the essential data you need without the overwhelming flood of apps and alerts. They track your health silently in the background, allowing you to focus on your actual life instead of your wrist. ## The Flagship: Withings ScanWatch 2

The ScanWatch 2 is the core of the current Withings lineup. Released as the successor to the highly popular original ScanWatch, this model refined the formula and added crucial new health sensors. It is available in two sizes (38mm and 42mm) to accommodate different wrist sizes, and it features a durable stainless steel case and a scratch-resistant sapphire glass crystal.

### Advanced Health Tracking

What makes the ScanWatch 2 special is its focus on proactive health monitoring. It is not just a fitness tracker; it is a miniature medical clinic. The watch features FDA-cleared electrocardiogram (ECG) functionality, allowing you to detect atrial fibrillation (AFib), a common and potentially dangerous heart arrhythmia. You simply touch the bezel of the watch for thirty seconds, and the watch records a medical-grade ECG that you can easily share with your doctor.

In addition to the ECG, the ScanWatch 2 includes a clinical-grade pulse oximeter (SpO2 sensor) to measure blood oxygen levels. This is particularly useful for detecting signs of sleep apnea or monitoring respiratory conditions. The watch tracks your breathing disturbances throughout the night, providing a comprehensive sleep quality score every morning.

One of the standout features introduced with the ScanWatch 2 is the TempTech24/7 module. This miniaturized heat flux sensor continuously tracks your baseline body temperature day and night. It does not just tell you your current temperature; it establishes your normal baseline and alerts you to any significant variations. This can be an early indicator of an oncoming illness, or it can be used to track menstrual cycles and predict ovulation windows with remarkable accuracy.

### Fitness and Activity Features

While health monitoring is the primary focus, the ScanWatch 2 is fully equipped for fitness tracking. It automatically recognizes dozens of different activities, from walking and running to swimming and cycling. The watch is water-resistant up to 50 meters, making it perfectly safe for the pool or the shower.

However, it is important to note a limitation: the ScanWatch 2 does not have built-in GPS. Instead, it relies on connected GPS, which means it uses your smartphone's location data to map your outdoor runs and bike rides. If you are a serious marathon runner who likes to leave your phone at home, this might be a dealbreaker. But for the casual athlete or the daily walker, the connected GPS works seamlessly as long as you have your phone in your pocket.

## The Premium Upgrade: Withings ScanWatch Nova

If the ScanWatch 2 is the practical daily driver, the ScanWatch Nova is the luxury upgrade. The Nova takes all the advanced sensors and internal technology of the ScanWatch 2 and wraps it in a stunning, diver-style watch body.

### Design and Materials

The ScanWatch Nova is undeniably gorgeous. It features a unidirectional rotating bezel, luminous hands and indices for visibility in the dark, and a highly polished stainless steel case. The watch face is protected by a thick layer of sapphire glass, ensuring it can withstand the rigors of daily wear without accumulating scratches.

The Nova is only available in a larger 42mm size, giving it a substantial presence on the wrist. It comes with a premium metal link bracelet (along with a comfortable fluoroelastomer sport band in the box), making it look and feel like a traditional luxury dive watch. It is available in stunning colorways, including a deep sunray blue and a rich brilliant green, which catch the light beautifully.

### Who is the Nova For?

Internally, the ScanWatch Nova is identical to the ScanWatch 2. It has the same ECG, the same SpO2 sensor, the same temperature tracking, and the exact same 30-day battery life. You are paying a premium purely for the design and the upgraded materials. If you work in a corporate environment where a plastic fitness tracker would look out of place, or if you simply appreciate the heft and aesthetic of a classic diver watch, the Nova is worth every penny.

## The Entry Level: Withings ScanWatch Light

For those who want the elegant look of a Withings hybrid but do not need the clinical-grade medical sensors, the company offers the ScanWatch Light. As the name suggests, this is a streamlined, more affordable version of the flagship watch.

### What Do You Lose?

The ScanWatch Light maintains the beautiful analog design, the tiny OLED screen, and the impressive 30-day battery life. It still tracks your daily steps, monitors your sleep stages, and provides notifications from your smartphone. However, to achieve a lower price point, Withings had to remove several hardware components.

The Light does not have the FDA-cleared ECG sensor, so it cannot detect AFib. It lacks the SpO2 sensor for blood oxygen monitoring, and it does not include the advanced TempTech24/7 module for continuous body temperature tracking. Furthermore, the crystal is made of Gorilla Glass rather than the virtually unscratchable sapphire crystal found on the more expensive models.

If you are a young, healthy individual who primarily wants a beautiful watch that counts steps and tracks sleep, the ScanWatch Light is a fantastic and economical choice. But if you are buying a Withings watch specifically for its proactive health monitoring capabilities, you are better off saving up for the ScanWatch 2.

## The Software Experience: The Withings App

Hardware is only half the equation. A health tracker is only as good as the software that interprets its data. Fortunately, the Withings app is one of the best in the business. It is clean, intuitive, and expertly designed to present complex medical data in a way that is easy to understand.

The app provides a daily dashboard that shows your activity, sleep quality, heart rate trends, and temperature variations. It offers actionable insights, gently nudging you to go to bed earlier or to take a walk to hit your daily step goal. The app also generates comprehensive health reports in PDF format, which you can easily print or email directly to your primary care physician before your annual checkup.

### The Withings+ Subscription

Like many hardware companies in 2026, Withings has introduced an optional premium subscription service called Withings+. While you can absolutely use any ScanWatch without paying a monthly fee (all your raw health data and basic metrics are always free), Withings+ offers additional layers of analysis and coaching.

The subscription unlocks advanced "Health Improvement Scores," targeted workout programs, and highly personalized daily missions based on your specific biomarker trends. Whether this subscription is worth the cost depends entirely on how much guidance you need. If you are highly self-motivated, the free tier is more than sufficient.

## The Rumor Mill: When Will We See a New Withings Watch?

As we move through the summer of 2026, the big question on everyone's mind is: when will Withings release a new watch? We have had the ScanWatch 2, Nova, and Light for quite some time now, and technology enthusiasts are eager for an upgrade. Will we see a ScanWatch 3 before the end of the year?

### Historical Release Cycles

To predict the future, we must look at the past. Withings does not follow the frantic annual release cycle of companies like Apple or Samsung. Because their watches are focused on long-term health tracking and classic design, they tend to have much longer product lifespans.

The original Withings ScanWatch was announced in early 2020 (though regulatory approvals delayed its release in certain regions like the United States). The successor, the ScanWatch 2, was officially unveiled in September 2023. This gives us a historical release cadence of roughly three years for major flagship models.

Following this mathematical logic, the next major leap in the ScanWatch lineage (the hypothetical ScanWatch 3) would be due in late 2026. September has traditionally been a popular month for consumer electronics announcements (coinciding with the IFA trade show in Berlin), making September 2026 a prime candidate for a potential Withings hardware reveal.

### What Fans Are Hoping For

While there are no official leaks confirming the specifications of a new model, the Withings community has been vocal about what they want to see in the next iteration.

1.  **NFC for Contactless Payments:** This is arguably the most requested feature. In a world where almost everyone pays for groceries and coffee with a tap of their wrist, the absence of NFC on current Withings watches is a glaring omission. Adding contactless payment support would make the watch infinitely more useful for daily errands.
2.  **Native Built-in GPS:** While connected GPS is fine for many, serious runners and cyclists want to be able to leave their phones behind. Adding native GPS would require careful engineering to avoid destroying the legendary 30-day battery life, but it would make the watch a true competitor to dedicated sports watches.
3.  **Improved OLED Screen:** The tiny circular screen on the current models is functional, but it can be difficult to read in bright, direct sunlight. Fans are hoping for a slightly larger, higher-resolution OLED display that can show more text at a glance while remaining invisible when turned off.
4.  **Lighter Titanium Options:** While the stainless steel bodies are beautiful, they are quite heavy. A brushed titanium version of the standard ScanWatch would provide excellent durability while significantly reducing the weight on the wrist, making it much more comfortable for sleep tracking.

### Official Statements

It is important to emphasize that as of July 2026, Withings has made absolutely no official announcements regarding a new smartwatch. Customer support representatives continue to point buyers toward the current ScanWatch 2 and Nova models, and the company has spent the early part of the year focusing on its smart scales and home health hubs rather than wearables.

If you are currently in the market for a hybrid smartwatch, you face a classic technology dilemma. Do you buy the excellent ScanWatch 2 today, knowing that a replacement might be announced in a few months? Or do you wait indefinitely for a product that has not been confirmed?
