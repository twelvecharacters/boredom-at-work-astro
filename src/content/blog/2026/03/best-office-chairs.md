## What Makes a Good Office Chair?

Before diving into specific models, here's what actually matters for all-day comfort.

### Must-Have Features

| Feature | Why It Matters | What to Look For |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| **Adjustable lumbar support** | Supports your lower back curve | Height + depth adjustment |
| **Seat height adjustment** | Feet flat on floor, thighs parallel | Pneumatic lift, easy to reach |
| **Armrest adjustability** | Prevents shoulder strain | At minimum height; ideally 4D |
| **Seat depth adjustment** | Fits different leg lengths | Sliding seat pan |
| **Recline with tension control** | Allows movement, reduces pressure | Synchro-tilt mechanism |

### Nice-to-Have Features

- Headrest (for reclining, video calls)
- Mesh back (breathability)
- Forward tilt (for focused work)
- Adjustable seat angle
- Lockable recline positions

### What Doesn't Matter Much

- "Gaming" aesthetics
- Built-in speakers/vibration
- Cup holders
- RGB lighting
- Brand collaborations (they're just skins)

## Office Chair Setup Tips

Once you get your chair, here's how to set it up properly.

### Proper Seated Position

| Body Part | Correct Position |
|-----------|------------------|
| Feet | Flat on floor (or footrest) |
| Knees | 90° angle, slightly below hips |
| Thighs | Parallel to floor |
| Back | Fully supported by backrest |
| Lumbar | Curve supported, not strained |
| Arms | 90° angle, resting on armrests |
| Eyes | Top of monitor at eye level |

### Adjustment Order

Set up your chair in this order:
1. **Seat height** — Feet flat, thighs parallel
2. **Seat depth** — 2-3 fingers between seat edge and back of knee
3. **Lumbar support** — In the curve of your lower back
4. **Armrests** — Shoulders relaxed, elbows at 90°
5. **Recline tension** — Supports you without pushing back

### The 20-20-20 Rule

Don't sit all day, even in the best chair:
- Every **20 minutes**, look at something **20 feet** away for **20 seconds**
- Stand up and move every hour
- Alternate sitting and standing if you have a standing desk

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Buying without trying** — If possible, test chairs in person. Ergonomics are personal.

2. **Wrong size** — Herman Miller Aeron comes in 3 sizes for a reason. Check manufacturer sizing guides.

3. **Ignoring lumbar support** — This is the #1 feature. Fixed pillows don't count.

4. **Setting and forgetting** — Readjust your chair as your body and work setup change.

5. **Skipping the break** — No chair eliminates the need for movement. Stand up regularly.

6. **Buying too cheap** — A $100 chair that lasts 2 years and hurts your back costs more than a $500 chair that lasts 10.
