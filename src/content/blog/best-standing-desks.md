## What Makes a Good Standing Desk?

Before we dive into specific models, here's what actually matters:

### Must-Have Features

| Feature | Why It Matters | What to Look For |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| **Dual motors** | Speed, stability, longevity | One motor per leg |
| **Weight capacity** | Supports your gear | 300+ lbs minimum |
| **Height range** | Fits your body | 25" to 50"+ |
| **Stability** | No wobble when typing | Steel frame, good reviews |
| **Warranty** | Confidence in quality | 10+ years frame, 5+ motors |

### Nice-to-Have Features

- Memory presets (save your perfect heights)
- Cable management (built-in trays or grommets)
- Anti-collision detection (prevents crushing stuff)
- Quiet motors (<50 dB)

### What Doesn't Matter Much

- RGB lighting
- Bluetooth apps
- Built-in USB chargers (often fail first)
- "Gaming" branding

## Standing Desk Setup Tips

Once you get your desk, here's how to use it properly:

### Ergonomic Height Settings

| Position | Monitor | Keyboard | Elbows |
|----------|---------|----------|--------|
| Sitting | Top at eye level | Elbows at 90° | Relaxed at sides |
| Standing | Top at eye level | Elbows at 90° | Relaxed at sides |

**Pro tip:** Your sitting and standing heights should keep your body position identical—only your legs change.

### The 30/30 Rule

Don't stand all day. Alternate:
- 30 minutes sitting
- 30 minutes standing
- Repeat

Standing all day is just as bad as sitting all day. Movement is the goal.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Buying single motor** — Always get dual motor. Always.

2. **Ignoring weight capacity** — Add up your monitors, laptop, speakers, etc. Leave 30% headroom.

3. **Forgetting cable management** — Cables must be long enough for full height range.

4. **Skipping the mat** — Standing on hard floors hurts. Get an anti-fatigue mat.

5. **Setting it and forgetting it** — Actually use the standing feature. Set phone reminders.
